    "## Join Counts\n",
    "\n",
    "Moran's $I$ and Geary's $C$ are designed for continuous variables. Once we dichotomize spatial data into binary high and low categories, a different global statistic is more natural: the **join count**, which evaluates whether like-valued neighbors occur more often than expected by chance.\n",
    "\n",
    "For a binary map where locations are classified as either **Black** ($B$, representing high values or presence) or **White** ($W$, representing low values or absence), each neighboring pair of zones forms a \"join\" mapped via our spatial weights object. Every join falls into one of three mutually exclusive categories:\n",
    "- **BB**: both neighbors are in the high-value category (Black-Black)\n",
    "- **WW**: both neighbors are in the low-value category (White-White)\n",
    "- **BW**: one neighbor is high-value and the other is low-value (Black-White)\n",
    "\n",
    "Let $x_i \\in \\{0, 1\\}$ be the binary indicator for location $i$, where $x_i = 1$ if the location is Black ($B$) and $x_i = 0$ if the location is White ($W$). The count of Black-Black (BB) joins is defined as:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$BB = \\frac{1}{2} \\sum_{i=1}^{n} \\sum_{j=1}^{n} w_{ij} x_i x_j$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where $w_{ij}$ is the binary spatial weight expressing the neighbor relationship between locations $i$ and $j$. Similarly, White-White (WW) and Black-White (BW) joins are formulated as:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$WW = \\frac{1}{2} \\sum_{i=1}^{n} \\sum_{j=1}^{n} w_{ij} (1 - x_i)(1 - x_j)$$\n",
    "\n",
    "$$BW = \\frac{1}{2} \\sum_{i=1}^{n} \\sum_{j=1}^{n} w_{ij} [x_i(1 - x_j) + x_j(1 - x_i)]$$\n",
    "\n",
    "The total number of joins in the network is given by half the sum of the spatial weights matrix elements ($S_0$), which completely exhausts all structural possibilities:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$K = \\frac{S_0}{2} = BB + WW + BW$$"
   ]

    "## Inference\n",
    "\n",
    "Inference about join counts evaluates whether the observed number of joins deviates significantly from what we would expect under the null hypothesis of Complete Spatial Randomness (CSR). \n",
    "\n",
    "To derive the analytical expected values and variances for statistical inference, we define structural constants based on the topology of the weights matrix $W$:\n",
    "\n",
    "* $S_0 = \\sum_{i=1}^n \\sum_{j=1}^n w_{ij}$ (The sum of all spatial weights)\n",
    "* $S_1 = \\frac{1}{2} \\sum_{i=1}^n \\sum_{j=1}^n (w_{ij} + w_{ji})^2$\n",
    "* $S_2 = \\sum_{i=1}^n \\left( \\sum_{j=1}^n w_{ij} + \\sum_{j=1}^n w_{ji} \\right)^2$\n",
    "\n",
    "Let $B = \\sum_{i=1}^n x_i$ be the total count of Black zones, and $W = n - B$ be the total count of White zones.\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### Free Sampling (The Normality Assumption)\n",
    "\n",
    "Under free sampling, it is assumed that each zone has an independent probability $p = B/n$ of being assigned a Black label, corresponding to sampling with replacement from a population.\n",
    "\n",
    "#### Expected Value\n",
    "The expected number of Black-Black joins depends entirely on the total network joins and the global probability $p$:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$E_N(BB) = \\frac{S_0}{2} p^2$$\n",
    "\n",
    "#### Variance\n",
    "The analytical variance under free sampling isolates interaction loops using structural constants $S_1$ and $S_2$:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$Var_N(BB) = \\frac{1}{4} p^2 (1 - p) \\left[ S_1(1 - p) + S_2 p \\right]$$\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### Non-Free Sampling (The Randomization Assumption)\n",
    "\n",
    "Under non-free sampling (randomization), the total counts of Black ($B$) and White ($W$) zones are treated as fixed constants. The observed labels are randomly shuffled across the spatial units, corresponding to sampling without replacement. \n",
    "\n",
    "To formalize this, let $n^{(k)} = n(n-1)\\dots(n-k+1)$ and $B^{(k)} = B(B-1)\\dots(B-k+1)$.\n",
    "\n",
    "#### Expected Value\n",
    "Because the expectation across all permutations averages out the spatial structure while respecting the exact counts, the expected value is:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$E_R(BB) = \\frac{S_0}{2} \\frac{B^{(2)}}{n^{(2)}} = \\frac{S_0 B(B-1)}{2n(n-1)}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "#### Variance\n",
    "The analytical variance under randomization is more complex as it accounts for joint multi-zone edge interaction parameters:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$Var_R(BB) = \\frac{S_1}{4} \\left[ \\frac{B^{(2)}}{n^{(2)}} - 2\\frac{B^{(3)}}{n^{(3)}} + \\frac{B^{(4)}}{n^{(4)}} \\right] + \\frac{S_2}{4} \\left[ \\frac{B^{(3)}}{n^{(3)}} - \\frac{B^{(4)}}{n^{(4)}} \\right] + \\frac{S_0^2}{4} \\frac{B^{(4)}}{n^{(4)}} - \\left[E_R(BB)\\right]^2$$\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "### Asymptotic Inference (Z-score)\n",
    "\n",
    "For practical execution with large datasets, these analytical moments can be used to construct an asymptotic standard normal $Z$-score:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$Z(BB) = \\frac{BB - E(BB)}{\\sqrt{Var(BB)}}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "* **Positive $Z(BB)$:** Indicates a significant spatial cluster of **similar high values** (positive spatial autocorrelation).\n",
    "* **Negative $Z(BB)$:** Indicates a significant spatial dispersion or alternating regular spacing of categories."
   ]
